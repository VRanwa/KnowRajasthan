Know Rajasthan

About Rajasthan

Rajasthan is a state in northern India. It is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning architecture. The state has numerous tourist attractions, including majestic palaces, forts, and desert landscapes.

Popular Cities

  • Jaipur

    • Jaipur, also known as the "Pink City," is the capital of Rajasthan. It is a vibrant city renowned for its magnificent palaces, majestic forts, and rich cultural heritage. The city is a perfect blend of history, architecture, and modernity. Jaipur is home to iconic landmarks such as the Hawa Mahal, Amer Fort, and City Palace, which showcase the grandeur and opulence of the Rajput era. The Hawa Mahal, with its intricate honeycomb-like fa√ßade, is a marvel of Rajput architecture. The Amer Fort, situated on a hilltop, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and boasts impressive structures like the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace) and Diwan-e-Khas (Hall of Private Audience). The City Palace, a magnificent complex of palaces, courtyards, and gardens, provides a glimpse into the royal heritage of Jaipur.

  • Jodhpur

    • Jodhpur, known as the "Blue City" for its vibrant blue-painted houses, is a captivating destination in Rajasthan. This historic city is famous for its magnificent forts, grand palaces, and rich cultural heritage. The most iconic landmark in Jodhpur is the majestic Mehrangarh Fort. Perched on a hilltop, the fort offers panoramic views of the city. Its massive walls and intricate architecture are a testament to the skill and craftsmanship of the bygone era. Inside the fort, you can explore palaces like the Moti Mahal (Pearl Palace), Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), and Phool Mahal (Flower Palace), adorned with exquisite artwork and delicate carvings.

  • Udaipur

    • Udaipur, often referred to as the "City of Lakes" or the "Venice of the East," is a breathtaking destination nestled amidst the Aravalli Hills in Rajasthan. Known for its romantic ambiance, serene lakes, and magnificent palaces, Udaipur is a true gem of Rajasthan. The most iconic landmark in Udaipur is the majestic Lake Palace (Jag Niwas). Located on an island in Lake Pichola, this enchanting palace appears to float on the shimmering waters. With its intricate architecture, exquisite marble work, and picturesque surroundings, the Lake Palace has been featured in numerous movies and is a symbol of Udaipur's charm.

  • Jaisalmer

    • Jaisalmer, also known as the "Golden City," is a mesmerizing destination located in the heart of the Thar Desert in Rajasthan. This ancient city is renowned for its golden sandstone architecture, magnificent forts, and rich cultural heritage. The most iconic landmark in Jaisalmer is the majestic Jaisalmer Fort, also known as Sonar Qila or the Golden Fort. Rising like a mirage from the surrounding desert, this massive fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its intricate carvings, narrow winding lanes, and stunning havelis (mansions) transport visitors to a bygone era. Inside the fort, you can explore palaces, temples, and enjoy panoramic views of the city and the vast desert landscape.

  • Ajmer

    • Ajmer, located in the heart of Rajasthan, is a city steeped in history and spirituality. Nestled at the foot of the Aravalli Hills, Ajmer is known for its rich cultural heritage, ancient monuments, and religious significance. One of the most prominent attractions in Ajmer is the revered Ajmer Sharif Dargah, a Sufi shrine dedicated to the sufi saint Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti. Pilgrims from different faiths visit this shrine to seek blessings and offer prayers. The dargah's magnificent architecture, with its white marble domes and intricate carvings, creates a serene ambiance. The spirituality and devotion that permeate the air make it a deeply moving experience for visitors.

Famous Forts

  • Amer Fort

    • Amer Fort, also known as Amber Fort, is an architectural marvel situated on a hilltop in Amer, a town located near Jaipur in Rajasthan. This majestic fort stands as a testament to the grandeur and opulence of the Rajput era. With its exquisite architecture, stunning views, and rich history, Amer Fort is a must-visit attraction in Rajasthan. Built in the 16th century by Raja Man Singh I, Amer Fort combines elements of Hindu and Mughal architectural styles. The fort is made of red sandstone and marble, creating a striking contrast against the surrounding rugged hills. Its elaborate facade, imposing gates, and intricately carved walls showcase the craftsmanship and artistic finesse of the bygone era.

  • Jaigarh Fort

    • Jaigarh Fort, also known as the "Victory Fort," is an impressive fortification located on a hilltop in the Aravalli Range near Jaipur, Rajasthan. Built in the 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaigarh Fort stands as a symbol of the military prowess and architectural brilliance of the Rajput rulers. Perched at an elevation of around 500 meters, Jaigarh Fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including the pink city of Jaipur and the stunning Amer Fort. The fort's strategic location atop the hills provided a vantage point for monitoring and defending the region.

  • Mehrangarh Fort

    • Mehrangarh Fort, located in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, is one of the most magnificent and formidable forts in India. Perched atop a 410-foot-high hill, the fort overlooks the "Blue City" of Jodhpur and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Built by Rao Jodha in the 15th century, Mehrangarh Fort is an architectural marvel that showcases the rich heritage and military might of the Rathore dynasty. The fort's massive walls, standing tall and impenetrable, are a testament to its defensive capabilities. The intricate detailing on the walls, featuring finely carved panels and elaborate latticework, is a testament to the exquisite craftsmanship of the artisans of that time.

  • Nahargarh Fort

    • Nahargarh Fort, located on the Aravalli hills overlooking the city of Jaipur in Rajasthan, is a captivating fortress that combines history, architecture, and panoramic views. Built in the 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Nahargarh Fort served as a defensive stronghold and a retreat for the royals. Perched at an elevation of approximately 700 feet, Nahargarh Fort offers stunning views of the Pink City, Jaipur. The fort's name, which translates to "abode of tigers," derives from a local legend that tells of a spirit named Nahar Singh Bhomia who haunted the area during the fort's construction.

  • Chittorgarh Fort

    • Chittorgarh Fort, located in the city of Chittorgarh in Rajasthan, is a historic fortress that stands as a testament to the valor, sacrifice, and rich heritage of Rajasthan. Spread across a sprawling area of over 700 acres, Chittorgarh Fort is one of the largest forts in India and holds immense historical significance. Founded in the 7th century by the Maurya dynasty, Chittorgarh Fort has witnessed numerous battles, sieges, and heroic acts of bravery throughout its history. The fort is known for its towering walls, majestic gates, magnificent palaces, intricate temples, and reservoirs that showcase the architectural brilliance of the Rajput era.

Places to Visit

  • Pushkar

    Pushkar is a holy city famous for its annual Pushkar Camel Fair and the Brahma Temple. It is located on the banks of the Pushkar Lake and attracts pilgrims and tourists from around the world.

  • Ranthambore National Park

    Ranthambore National Park is a renowned wildlife sanctuary known for its population of royal Bengal tigers. Visitors can enjoy wildlife safaris and spot various other animals and bird species in their natural habitat.

  • Mount Abu

    Mount Abu is the only hill station in Rajasthan and offers a refreshing retreat from the heat of the plains. It is home to the famous Dilwara Temples, Nakki Lake, and offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills.
